Identity + Nationality + Job (Combined)

    I am an Indian student, and Mr. Miller is an American company employee.

    Ms. Sato is Japanese, but she is not a teacher.

    He is a Chinese engineer, and he is also a company employee.

    I am not a teacher; I am a student.

    She is a Japanese language student, not an engineer.

Contrast Using Negation

Mr. Wang is not Japanese; he is Chinese.

    I am not an employee; I am a student.

    She is not an Indian; she is Japanese.

    He is not a teacher; he is an engineer.

    Ms. Tanaka is not a student; she is a company employee.

    “Also / Too” (も) with Structure

    I am a student. She is also a student.

    Mr. Miller is an employee. Mr. Sato is also an employee.

    I am Indian. He is also Indian.

    She is a teacher. I am also a teacher.

Possession + Identity (の)

    This is the Japanese teacher’s book.

    Is this the student’s pen?
