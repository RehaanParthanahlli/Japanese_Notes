JAPANESE — PRACTICAL SPEAKING (FOR DAILY USE)

-----------------------------------------------
CORE PARTICLES (remember these first)

wa   = topic marker (“about …”)
ka   = question marker (?)
ga   = highlights the thing itself
o-   = polite prefix (softens the word)
desu = is / am (polite)

Example:
Watashi wa Rehaan desu.
= I am Rehaan.

-----------------------------------------------
GREETINGS

Ohayō                 = Good morning
Konnichiwa            = Hello
Arigatō               = Thank you
Arigatō gozaimasu     = Thank you (very polite)
Hai                   = Yes
Iie                   = No

-----------------------------------------------
O — POLITE PREFIX (IMPORTANT)

“o-” adds respect / softness.

Examples:
Ogenki      = in good health (polite)
Ocha        = tea (polite way)
Okane       = money (polite way)

Ogenki desu ka?
= How are you?
(literally: “Are you well — politely?”)

-----------------------------------------------
SELF-INTRODUCTION

Watashi wa Rehaan desu.
= I am Rehaan.

Onamae wa nan desu ka?
= What is your name?

Dōzo yoroshiku.
= Nice to meet you.

India kara kimashita.
= I came from India.

-----------------------------------------------
QUESTIONS

Kore wa pen desu ka?
= Is this a pen?

Kore wa nan desu ka?
= What is this?

-----------------------------------------------
WHERE / LOCATION

... wa doko desu ka?
= Where is ... ?

Toire wa doko desu ka?
= Where is the toilet?

Eki wa doko desu ka?
= Where is the station?

Koko desu.   = Here.
Asoko desu.  = There.

-----------------------------------------------
WANT / NOT WANT

... ga hoshii desu
= I want ...

Mizu ga hoshii desu.
= I want water.

... wa hoshikunai desu
= I don’t want ...

-----------------------------------------------
UNDERSTANDING

Wakarimasu
= I understand

Wakarimasen
= I don’t understand

Sumimasen, wakarimasen
= Sorry, I didn’t understand.

-----------------------------------------------
POLITE REQUESTS

Onegaishimasu
= Please (general request)

Mō ichido onegaishimasu
= Please say it again.

Yukkuri hanashite kudasai
= Please speak slowly.

-----------------------------------------------
MONEY / SHOPPING

Ikura desu ka?
= How much is it?

Takai desu.
= It’s expensive.

Yasui desu.
= It’s cheap.

Sukoshi dake kudasai.
= Just a little, please.

-----------------------------------------------
FOOD / DAILY LIFE

Ocha ga hoshii desu.
= I want tea.

Kōhī ga hoshii desu.
= I want coffee.

Kare wa oishii desu.
= The curry is tasty.

-----------------------------------------------
REMEMBER (20% RULES)

1) [TOPIC] wa ... desu
2) Add ka → becomes a question
3) o- makes words polite
4) Use kudasai with actions (“please do …”)
5) Speak out loud — don’t just read
