====================================================================
JAPANESE GRAMMAR — DEEP DIVE INTO CORE WORD TYPES
(Pronouns • Helping verbs • Particles • Adverbs • Conjunctions)
====================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------
0) BIG PICTURE (DO NOT SKIP)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
English builds meaning using:
• helper verbs
• fixed pronouns
• prepositions

Japanese builds meaning using:
• particles
• verb conjugation
• context

Many English categories DO NOT exist as separate words in Japanese.

====================================================================
1) PRONOUNS (I / YOU / HE / SHE / WE / THEY)
====================================================================

DEFINITION:
Pronouns replace nouns (I, you, he, she).

JAPANESE REALITY:
• Pronouns exist
• BUT are OPTIONAL
• Often DROPPED if context is clear

------------------------------------------------
I / ME / MY / MINE
------------------------------------------------

わたし = I / me

わたし は がくせい です。
I am a student.

これは わたし の ほん です。
This is my book.

その ほん は わたし の です。
That book is mine. (object known)

これは わたし の もの です。
This is mine. (object unknown)

せんせい は わたし に ほん を くれました。
The teacher gave me a book.

------------------------------------------------
YOU / YOUR / YOURS
------------------------------------------------

あなた = you (polite but distant)

あなた は がくせい です。
You are a student.

これは あなた の ほん です。
This is your book.

その ほん は あなた の です。
That book is yours.

せんせい は あなた に しつもん を しました。
The teacher asked you a question.

NATIVE JAPANESE (preferred):
リハーンさん は がくせい です。
(You) are a student.

------------------------------------------------
HE / SHE
------------------------------------------------

かれ = he
かのじょ = she

かれ は がくせい です。
He is a student.

かのじょ は せんせい です。
She is a teacher.

NATURAL JAPANESE (preferred):
あの人 は がくせい です。
That person is a student. (he/she)

------------------------------------------------
WE / THEY
------------------------------------------------

わたしたち は がくせい です。
We are students.

かれら は がくせい です。
They are students.

KEY RULE:
If English REQUIRES a pronoun,
Japanese often DROPS it.

====================================================================
2) HELPING VERBS (IS / AM / ARE / WAS / WERE)
====================================================================

ENGLISH:
I am
you are
he is
was / were

JAPANESE:
❌ NO verb "to be"
✅ ONE copula: です

------------------------------------------------
AM / IS / ARE
------------------------------------------------

わたし は がくせい です。
I am a student.

かれ は いそがしい です。
He is busy.

------------------------------------------------
WAS / WERE
------------------------------------------------

わたし は がくせい でした。
I was a student.

きのう は ひま でした。
Yesterday was free.

------------------------------------------------
NEGATIVE
------------------------------------------------

がくせい じゃない です。
(am/is not)

がくせい じゃなかった です。
(was not)

IMPORTANT:
Japanese does NOT change by person.
です works for everyone.

====================================================================
3) HELPING VERBS (HAVE / HAS / HAD)
====================================================================

English:
I have eaten
I had eaten

Japanese:
❌ NO "have" helper
✅ COMPLETION is shown by verb form

------------------------------------------------
PAST = COMPLETED
------------------------------------------------

ごはん を たべました。
I ate / I have eaten.

------------------------------------------------
ONGOING STATE
------------------------------------------------

ごはん を たべて います。
I am eating / I have been eating.

KEY RULE:
Japanese asks:
"Is it completed or not?"

NOT:
"Is it present perfect?"

====================================================================
4) MODAL HELPERS (CAN / MUST / MIGHT / SHOULD)
====================================================================

English uses separate helper verbs.
Japanese ATTACHES meaning to verbs.

------------------------------------------------
CAN
------------------------------------------------

たべられる
= can eat

にほんご が はなせます。
I can speak Japanese.

------------------------------------------------
MUST / HAVE TO
------------------------------------------------

いかなければ なりません。
must go

しゅくだい を しなければ なりません。
I must do homework.

------------------------------------------------
MIGHT / MAY
------------------------------------------------

あめ が ふる かも しれません。
It might rain.

------------------------------------------------
SHOULD
------------------------------------------------

はやく ねた ほう が いい です。
You should sleep early.

====================================================================
5) PARTICLES (REPLACE PREPOSITIONS)
====================================================================

DEFINITION:
Particles show the ROLE of a word.

ENGLISH:
in / on / at / to / from / with

JAPANESE:
Particles AFTER nouns.

------------------------------------------------
は (topic)
------------------------------------------------
わたし は がくせい です。
As for me, I am a student.

------------------------------------------------
を (object)
------------------------------------------------
ほん を よみます。
I read a book.

------------------------------------------------
に (to / time / target)
------------------------------------------------
がっこう に いきます。
I go to school.

------------------------------------------------
で (place of action)
------------------------------------------------
がっこう で べんきょう します。
I study at school.

------------------------------------------------
から / まで
------------------------------------------------
いえ から がっこう まで いきます。
I go from home to school.

====================================================================
6) ADVERBS (HERE / THERE / THIS / THAT / HOW)
====================================================================

------------------------------------------------
PLACE
------------------------------------------------
ここ   = here
そこ   = there (near you)
あそこ = over there
どこ   = where

トイレ は どこ です か。
Where is the toilet?

------------------------------------------------
THIS / THAT (OBJECTS)
------------------------------------------------
これ = this
それ = that
あれ = that (far)

これは ペン です。
This is a pen.

------------------------------------------------
MANNER / DEGREE
------------------------------------------------
とても = very
すこし = a little
よく   = often
もう   = already
まだ   = still / not yet

よく にほんご を べんきょう します。
I often study Japanese.

====================================================================
7) CONJUNCTIONS (AND / BUT / SO / BECAUSE)
====================================================================

------------------------------------------------
WORD CONJUNCTIONS
------------------------------------------------

そして = and
でも   = but
だから = so
しかし = however

あつい です。だから、みず を のみます。
It is hot, so I drink water.

------------------------------------------------
VERB CONNECTING (MORE IMPORTANT)
------------------------------------------------

て-form = and / then / because

ごはん を たべて、ねました。
I ate and slept.

====================================================================
FINAL MASTER SUMMARY
====================================================================

English:
• pronouns mandatory
• helper verbs everywhere
• prepositions before nouns

Japanese:
• pronouns optional
• helpers attach to verbs
• particles after nouns
• context does the work

If you stop translating English grammar,
Japanese becomes SIMPLE.
